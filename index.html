Data Cleanse, Transformation and Analysis:

  • Genbank Data Disambiguation

(http://bit.ly/2nzC34l)

There are millions of authors and inventors who submit references to NCBI’s GenBank, and there are a wide range of alternate spellings of a person’s or institution’s name. Typos and misspellings of names are also common in bibliographic data.

The goal of this disambiguation project is to link all of these alternate spellings of institutional or individual names without incorrectly linking similar names referring to distinct entities. This is a difficult task, as there are millions of names to disambiguate (making pairwise comparisons of the full dataset computationally expensive) and an evaluation of how likely two names on patents are to be the same entity is not known a priori and often relies on machine learning techniques.

To effectively alleviate these difficulties, I wrote and implemented an innovative co-author matching method, and managed to disambiguate 50% of the records with high confidence.
